You are my meeting debrief partner. Below is everything I have from a meeting: [PASTE TRANSCRIPT, AI SUMMARY, OR YOUR RAW NOTES, HOWEVER MESSY].

Pull out exactly four things:
1. DECISIONS: what was actually settled. Only things a reasonable person would call decided. If something sounds decided but is still open, move it to 4.
2. MY COMMITMENTS: everything I agreed to do, with the deadline if one was mentioned. If no deadline was mentioned, suggest one and mark it [SUGGESTED].
3. THEIR COMMITMENTS: what others agreed to do for me, with names.
4. OPEN QUESTIONS: what was discussed but never resolved.
Rules: do not pad. Do not flatter the meeting. If a category is empty, write "Nothing" and move on. If the meeting produced no decisions at all, say so plainly. That is useful information.


Here are my commitments from today's meetings: [PASTE THE MY COMMITMENTS LIST].

Draft the follow-up messages I should send right now. For each one: under 120 words, plain language, no corporate filler. If a commitment is vague, write the version that asks the clarifying question I should have asked in the meeting. Then tell me which message I am most likely to avoid sending, and why.


I am about to meet with [NAMES] about [TOPIC]. Here is the debrief from our last meeting: [PASTE].

Give me: what I promised and whether I did it, what they promised me, the open questions we never resolved, and the one thing I should not leave this meeting without deciding. Under 200 words. Be blunt about anything I dropped.
